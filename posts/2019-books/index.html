Top 5 reads of 2019

2020-01-02

New year, new gear. Time to reflect on the lessons learned during 2019, a year that I remember from its warm and pleasant summer, Greta Thunberg and raising awareness of climate change, Brexit drama, and the “stable ingenuity” of Donald Trump.

On a more personal note, one of the highlights of the year was that I left Europe for the first time in my life for a summer holiday in California, USA. I traveled alone, even though a travel companion or a gang would have been awesome. Luckily there's one thing a solo traveller can do at their leisure that's not so convenient when travelling in company. And that is to binge-read books.

I read physical books quite infrequently in my 20's because I was hooked on Wikipedia and my studies took their toll, too. However, I have picked up reading again in recent years. One of the sources of happiness I have discovered is reading in my bed at night by the light of a dim lamp. I have a hunch this might be the hygge the Danish talk about.

I read just for the sake of reading, often times just by opening a book in the middle and following along for a chapter or two. Poetry, mathematics and philosophy work best for this purpose. They make you might drowsy, too. Every once in a while a captivating piece of work comes along that I simply devour from head to tail. Here are 5 such books that I read in 2019 and my “review” about them.

1. Juha Hurme: Niemi (The Cape)

I read Juha Hurme's Niemi in the desert surroundings of Joshua Tree, CA, and boy was it a good book. In it, Hurma lays out the history of Finland, starting from the Big Bang, dinosaurs and tectonic movements. Think of the book as sort of the literary version of Right Here, Right Now by Fatboy Slim but with a Finnish twist.
